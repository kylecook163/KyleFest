Privacy Policy

This page is written to work both as your public website privacy section and as the policy users can access from the App Store.

Your party. Your people. Your privacy.

In short: KyleFest does not sell your data, does not serve ads, and does not share your information with third-party advertisers. Most of your data stays on your device. The only data stored on our servers is your account profile if you create one. Party activity, messages, and game data are not persisted after you leave.

Last updated: April 2026 Intended for 18+ No tracking

1. What we collect

  • Account info: if you create an account, we store your email address, display name, and a hashed password via Supabase. If you use Sign in with Apple, we receive the identity token Apple provides.
  • Profile data: your display name and profile photo are stored locally on your device. If you have an account, your display name is also stored so it can sync across devices.
  • Lifetime stats: wins, losses, Elo rating, CookPoints, and similar gameplay statistics may be stored locally and may sync through legacy Apple sign-in backup flows.
  • Moderation preferences: mute, hide, and block relationships are stored locally on your device.

2. Party and social data

  • When you join a party, your display name, avatar, and profile are shared with other party members over the local network.
  • Chat messages, game sessions, bets, polls, and shared photos or videos are transmitted in real time between devices on the same network.
  • This party data lives in memory while the app is running and is not persisted to a server after you leave the party.
  • Direct messages between party members use end-to-end encryption, and the encryption keys stay on your device.

3. How we use your data

  • To identify you in parties and sync your profile across devices
  • To track achievements, leaderboards, Elo ratings, and other gameplay features
  • To apply your local moderation settings like mute, hide, and block
  • We do not sell your data, serve ads, or share your information with third-party advertisers

4. Where data is stored

  • On your device: profile info, avatar, moderation preferences, drink preferences, and notification settings
  • In iOS Keychain: encryption keys and secure credential-related items
  • In Supabase: if you create an account, your email, display name, and authentication credentials
  • In memory only: chat, game state, bets, party rosters, and shared media during a live party session

5. Account deletion

  • You can request account deletion from Settings → Data & Account → Delete Account.
  • Local data like your profile, avatar, and moderation preferences is cleared from your device when you delete your account.
  • Server-side deletion of your Supabase profile and authentication record is processed as part of the deletion flow.
  • In-memory party data is not persisted after you leave a party, so there is nothing additional to delete for session-only data.

6. Data shared with other users

  • While in a party, other members can see your display name, profile photo, game activity, chat messages, bets, poll votes, and shared media.
  • Your email address is never shared with other users.
  • Moderation actions you take are private to your device and are not visible to the person you moderated.

7. Third-party services

  • Supabase is used for account authentication and profile storage.
  • Apple services may be used for Sign in with Apple and legacy iCloud or CloudKit backup flows.
  • We do not integrate analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

8. Security

  • Peer-to-peer connections use encrypted transport.
  • Direct messages use end-to-end encryption and device-generated keys stored securely on your device.
  • We do not store plaintext passwords.

9. Voice calling

Voice calls occur only between devices on the same local Wi-Fi network during an active party session. Voice data is transmitted peer-to-peer and is not routed through a centralized server. Calls are not recorded, stored, or persisted by the app or our servers.

10. Data retention and deletion

  • Party activity is held in memory during a session and is automatically deleted when you leave the party or close the app.
  • Local profile data, moderation preferences, and game stats are retained until you delete your account or uninstall the app.
  • Supabase account data is deleted within 30 days of an account deletion request.

11. Data export

You can export your account data from Settings → Data & Account → Export Data. The export contains your profile, stats, and preferences. It does not include in-memory party data because that session data is ephemeral.

12. No tracking or analytics

  • KyleFest does not use analytics, tracking, or behavioral monitoring SDKs.
  • We do not track your behavior, build advertising profiles, or sell your data.

13. Your rights

  • You can access, export, and delete your data at any time through Settings.
  • For privacy-related requests, including access, correction, or deletion, contact us directly.

14. Contact

Questions about this policy or the app can be sent to KyleFestApp@gmail.com.

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to the most common questions about what KyleFest is, how it works, and what kind of experience it is built for.

What is KyleFest?

KyleFest is a local-first party app that combines chat, shared media, games, polls, party tools, and quick navigation into one experience for groups hanging out together.

Is KyleFest a public social network?

No. It is designed around private party sessions and group experiences rather than public feeds or open discovery.

What can you do in the app?

You can message the group, send DMs, use the camera, view shared photos and videos, track games, run polls, manage party activity, and use the Home dashboard to keep up with everything.

Who is the app for?

KyleFest is built for friend groups, parties, cabins, weekends away, events, and other local group hangouts where people want one shared app experience.

Will an Android version come out?

No — KyleFest is built for iOS. Maybe one day… but right now we’re keeping it in the Apple ecosystem.

Why is it local-first?

Because the app is meant to feel immediate and connected to the people around you right now, not to a giant public network of strangers.
